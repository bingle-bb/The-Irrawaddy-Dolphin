Overview

The Irrawaddy dolphin is a euryhaline oceanic dolphin found near sea coasts, estuaries, and rivers in the Bay of Bengal and Southeast Asia. It closely resembles the Australian snubfin dolphin, identified as a separate species in 2005.

Why We Should Protect Them?

  • Environmental Balance: Irrawaddy dolphins help maintain a healthy aquatic ecosystem by regulating fish populations.
  • Biodiversity Preservation: Their survival supports ecosystem diversity, preventing the loss of a unique species.
  • Cultural Significance: Many local communities view these dolphins as symbols of good fortune and harmony.
  • Economic Importance: They attract eco-tourism, providing sustainable income for local communities.
  • Indicator of River Health: Their presence reflects a clean and thriving water system.
  • Legal and Moral Responsibility: As an endangered species, it is our duty to combat threats like habitat destruction and pollution.
